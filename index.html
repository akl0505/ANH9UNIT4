Facts about Angkor Wat

1. Biggest Religious Building

Angkor Wat is the largest religious building in the world, first built as a Hindu temple and later becoming a Buddhist temple.

2. Built in the 12th Century

It was constructed in the early 1100s by King Suryavarman II, taking around 30 years to complete.

3. Amazing Carvings

The temple is known for its beautiful stone carvings that depict stories from Hindu mythology.

4. Faces West

Unlike most temples, Angkor Wat faces west. This may be because the king wanted it as his tomb.

5. Symbol of Cambodia

Angkor Wat is so important that it appears on Cambodia's national flag.

6. Rediscovered by the West

Western countries learned about Angkor Wat in the 1800s, thanks to a French explorer who documented it.

7. Difficult Building Work

Stones were transported from over 50 km away, using rivers and canals to move them.

8. UNESCO Site

In 1992, it became a UNESCO World Heritage Site, helping protect it for future generations.

9. Aligned with Stars

Angkor Wat lines up with certain star events, like the spring equinox, adding to its special significance.

10. Popular Tourist Attraction

Millions of people visit Angkor Wat every year, making it one of the world's top tourist spots.
